# Security
How am I meant to trust that my ID will be safe, when it is uploaded to one of these identity verification websites.  The idea behind them is that there isn't any ID or personal information stored, that everything is verified and then deleted.  If that is the case, why are there data breaches with that information stored: [1](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2025/oct/09/hack-age-verification-firm-discord-users-id-photos), [2](https://gizmodo.com/identity-verification-firm-used-by-x-tiktok-and-uber-1851562934)?

Weren't we all taught to not upload private information to the internet, to keep ourselves safe by not giving out ID?  Yet google is attempting to make approved operating systems a [requirement to use the internet](https://cybernews.com/privacy/google-qr-code-recaptcha-requires-approved-phone/), which means that the aim is that people cannot get around the identity verification, while still being able to use money or do their job.
